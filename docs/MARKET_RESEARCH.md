## Current Solutions & Their Limitations

### 1. Grammarly Authorship

**What it does:** Tracks writing process, shows % original vs AI
**Limitations:**
- Only works if you write IN Grammarly
- Doesn't help if you used ChatGPT/Claude directly
- TRACKS usage, doesn't GENERATE disclosure statements
- Doesn't coach on prompt quality

[Source: Grammarly Authorship](https://www.grammarly.com/authorship)

### 2. University Templates

**What they provide:** Fill-in-the-blank Word docs
**Limitations:**
- Tedious to complete
- 74% of students skip them
- No standardization across institutions
- No formatting for APA/MLA/Chicago

[Example: Newman University Template](https://newmanu.libguides.com/ai-disclosure)

### 3. Citation Generators (MyBib, Scribbr, etc.)

**What they do:** Generate citations for sources
**Limitations:**
- Don't generate AI disclosure statements
- Can't verify if AI-generated citations are real
- No prompt coaching

[Source: MyBib](https://www.mybib.com)

### 4. AI Detection Tools (Turnitin, GPTZero)

**What they do:** Detect AI-generated content
**Limitations:**
- Adversarial to students
- High false positive rates
- Universities abandoning them
- Don't help students use AI properly
